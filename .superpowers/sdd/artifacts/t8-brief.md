# T8 Brief — Challenger regret resolution + hypothesis registry wiring (M10 criteria 5+6, closing out M10)

## Spec text (docs/FORGE_PROPOSAL.md lines 1182–1183, read them directly for the authoritative wording)

Criterion 5 (the unfinished parts): once the challenger has `desk.challenger_min_decisions` (default 20) **labeled** decisions or `desk.challenger_max_days` (default 7) elapse, **a scheduler job compares mean labeled regret over the window**: challenger wins → promoted to `active` (incumbent → `inactive`); loses → `rejected`. Either way the outcome lands in `reflections` and resolves the cycle's hypotheses (criterion 6). Fully automatic — paper capital only.

Criterion 6: `hypotheses` table (already in data/schema.sql:182+; columns include claim, feature, direction, regime_context, predicted_effect, falsification_condition, status ∈ proposed|challenger|validated|falsified|inconclusive, effect_observed, created_at, resolved_at). Stage A registers rows; the challenger trial resolves them (predicted effect realized → `validated`; falsification condition met or challenger rejected → `falsified`; window expired without signal → `inconclusive`, with `effect_observed` recorded in all cases). The dossier includes the agent's own registry history, so it cannot re-propose its own falsified ideas without addressing the falsification.

## Deliverables

1. **Regret-based `resolve_challenger`** in store/specs.py: both sides = mean labeled regret over the trial window (challenger shadow rows vs incumbent executed rows, both `timestamp >= deployed_at`, joined to `decision_labels`). LOWER mean regret wins (regret = best-available − chosen). Handle no-labels-yet (not resolvable — don't promote/reject on zero evidence). Keep window scoping. Keep the promotion/rejection status mechanics (challenger → active + incumbent → inactive, or challenger → rejected).
2. **Resolution scheduler job** in forge.py: periodic job that finds agents with a deployed challenger, applies the min-labeled-decisions / max-days trigger (`check_challenger_resolution` logic; config from `desk.challenger_min_decisions`/`desk.challenger_max_days` — fail loudly if the desk section is missing, defaults 20/7 per spec), calls resolve_challenger when due, writes the outcome into the cycle's `reflections` row, and resolves the cycle's hypotheses. 3. **Hypothesis registration**: `run_reflection` Stage A persists parsed hypotheses via `register_hypotheses` (status `proposed`, linked to agent_id + reflection row); when the cycle's proposal deploys as challenger, its hypotheses move to status `challenger`.
4. **Hypothesis resolution**: on challenger resolution, `resolve_hypotheses` applies the spec semantics — predicted effect realized → `validated`; falsification condition met OR challenger rejected → `falsified`; window expired without signal → `inconclusive`; `effect_observed` recorded in ALL cases, `resolved_at` set. 6. **Agent page rendering**: agent_detail.html shows, for recent reflection cycles: the dossier digest (reflections.research_findings_json), hypothesis outcomes (per-cycle hypotheses with status/effect_observed), and the thesis/spec diffs (reflections.proposed_changes).
